Learning Things

In early 2018 I started this site to help myself think more effectively. I had spent a lot of time reading and studying, but was frustrated by how much I ended up forgetting.

As I began making a conscious effort to improve information retention, I heard about many different methodologies and eventually settled on a few, namely compressed knowledge and spaced repetition with a bias towards narrative and tree sources. This site's contents heavily reflect this; my notes are organized as full-text-searchable webpages, and my full Anki deck is published here regularly. The tradeoff of consuming a lower volume of quality material with an emphasis on thorough understanding has made learning, working, and building orders of magnitude more enjoyable, productive, and fulfilling.

While the primary purpose of this site is as a personal reference, it also ostensibly functions as a set of recommendations for anyone with shared interests, just like the many book lists and Twitter feeds I've trolled to find books and articles. The headers at the top are a good starting point. My generic star-rating system goes from 1 (waste of time) to 5 (legitimately life-changing in some form), with 3 being average. Each tag has its own page with items in descending rating order. If you know of some content you think I might enjoy, please shoot me an email!
